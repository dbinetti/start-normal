Aren't you worried about the virus?

Yes, obviously there is some apprehension. But the available evidence shows fairly clearly that while children do get the virus, their risk of serious illness or transmission to others is extremely rare, so the worry is tempered by data.

What about spreading the virus to adults?

Studies show that children carry viral loads equal to adults, but for whatever reason we don't see evidence of actual transmission. We have clear evidence of adults transmitting to children, and of adults transmitting to other adults; those cases can and have been tracked with great precision. Yet authorities have yet to find a single case of child to adult transmission anywhere in the world, even when looking closely at infected children who spent protracted periods with adults under close-contact scenarios. This doesn't mean it isn't possible, but as of yet the evidence of super-spreaders, or any spread for that matter, is non-existent.

Why sleepaway camps?

Starting with sleepaway camps makes sense since they are completely isolated. Thus, if children do turn out to be spreaders, then the actual incidence of infection can be completely contained within the camp. Residential camps are also closer environments, with children spending all day and sleeping together at night, and if there is no spread under these conditions then schools by comparison will be even lower. If sleepaways work, then perhaps we can extend to daily recreational camps to further test as well.

What if someone tests positive?

Ultimately the study design is up to the researchers, but the point is to measure and track the spread of Covid-19 as it would in a school environment. Medical professionals will be on hand to make appropriate decisions, but the point would be to maintain normalcy as much as possible.

How would it work for camp operators?

Camp operators would have legal protection under the program, allowing for normal operation with all activites and no restrictions on distance, shared items or personal protective equipment. Kids will be kids, and camps will be camps.

Who would pay for this?

As of now, particpants would function normally, including paying for the camp. If funding from government sources became available that would certainly be considered.
